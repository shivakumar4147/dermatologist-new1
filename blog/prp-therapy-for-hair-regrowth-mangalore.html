PRP and GFC Therapy for Hair Regrowth: A Comprehensive Medical Guide

By Dr. Prashanth R Kamath | March 8, 2024 | 11 min read

Hair restoration has entered a new era with the advent of regenerative medicine. No longer are patients limited to just topical medications or invasive surgery. Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP) and Growth Factor Concentrate (GFC) therapy have become the gold standard for non-surgical hair restoration. At our dermatology clinic in Mangaluru, we specialize in these advanced procedures to help patients reverse thinning and promote healthy, dense hair regrowth. In this guide, we explore the science behind these regenerative therapies and how they can transform your hair health.

1. The Science of Regenerative Hair Care

Regenerative hair care works by harnessing the body's natural healing potential. Your blood contains platelets, which are rich in growth factors—proteins that act as signaling molecules to trigger tissue repair and cell growth. When concentrated and injected into the scalp, these growth factors 'wake up' dormant hair follicles, extend the growth (anagen) phase of the hair cycle, and increase the blood supply to the hair bulb. This leads to thicker, stronger, and more resilient hair strands.

2. PRP vs. GFC: What is the Difference?

While both therapies utilize your own blood, the process of concentration differs:

  • PRP (Platelet-Rich Plasma): Involves centrifuging your blood to separate a platelet-rich layer, which is then re-injected. It contains a mix of platelets and some white blood cells.
  • GFC (Growth Factor Concentrate): An advanced, acellular version of PRP. In this process, the platelets are intentionally 'activated' in the lab to release their growth factors into a concentrate. This concentrate is then injected, providing a much higher and more standardized dose of active proteins for superior regrowth.

At our Mangaluru skin clinic, we offer both options and will recommend the best one based on the severity of your hair loss and your specific regrowth goals.

3. Who is a Good Candidate for PRP/GFC?

Regenerative therapies are highly effective for various types of hair loss, including:

  • Androgenetic Alopecia (Pattern Baldness): Both men and women with thinning hair can see significant density improvements.
  • Telogen Effluvium: Rapidly stabilizes shedding after stress, illness, or post-pregnancy.
  • Thinning Hair: Excellent for patients who want to improve the overall quality and volume of their hair.

It is most effective when hair follicles are still present; it cannot regrow hair in areas of complete, long-term baldness where follicles have scarred over.

4. The Clinical Procedure: What to Expect

A PRP or GFC session at Skin 360 follows a strict medical protocol:

  1. Blood Collection: A small amount of blood (similar to a routine blood test) is drawn from your arm.
  2. Concentration: The blood is processed in a high-speed centrifuge to isolate the growth factor-rich concentrate.
  3. Scalp Injection: The concentrate is injected into the thinning areas of the scalp using very fine needles. We use topical numbing agents to ensure the procedure is virtually painless.
  4. Recovery: There is absolutely no downtime. You can resume your normal routine, including hair washing, within 24 hours.

5. Timeline of Results and Maintenance

Hair growth is a slow biological process. Most patients follow a structured timeline:

  • Month 1-2: Reduction in active hair fall is often the first sign of improvement.
  • Month 3-4: Visible increase in hair diameter and texture.
  • Month 6+: Significant density improvement and better scalp coverage.

A typical course involves 3 to 6 sessions, spaced 4 weeks apart. Maintenance sessions every 6 months are recommended to sustain the density and health of the regrown hair.

7. Combining Regenerative Therapy with Medical Care

For the best results, we often combine PRP/GFC with medical therapies like Minoxidil, oral DHT blockers, or specialized nutritional supplements. This 'multi-modal' approach ensures that we address the hair loss from both the inside and the outside, leading to more sustainable and transformative outcomes.

Common Questions: PRP/GFC in Mangaluru

Is PRP therapy painful?

We prioritize patient comfort. We use high-quality numbing creams and very fine needles to ensure the procedure is as comfortable as possible. Most patients describe it as a minor 'pinprick' sensation.

Are there any side effects?

Since PRP and GFC use your own blood, there is virtually no risk of allergic reactions. You may experience mild redness or a slight 'fullness' in the scalp for a few hours, which is completely normal.

Can PRP replace a hair transplant?

PRP/GFC is a non-surgical treatment for thinning hair. While it can delay the need for a transplant by years, it cannot replace one in areas of total baldness. It is often used *after* a transplant to improve the survival of the new follicles.
