How to Stop Hair Loss Naturally and Medically: An Expert Guide

By Dr. Sarah Wilson | March 8, 2024 | 12 min read

Hair loss is a source of significant anxiety for both men and women. In a city like Mangaluru, factors such as humidity, hard water, and stress can further exacerbate hair shedding. At our dermatology clinic in Mangaluru, we believe that the most effective way to stop hair loss is a multi-modal approach that combines natural lifestyle adjustments with clinically proven medical treatments. In this guide, we break down how you can protect your hair density.

1. Identify the Type of Hair Loss First

Before you start any treatment, it's crucial to know what you're dealing with. Is it Androgenetic Alopecia (genetic thinning)? Telogen Effluvium (stress-induced shedding)? Or perhaps an autoimmune condition like Alopecia Areata? A hair specialist in Mangaluru uses tools like trichoscopy to look at your scalp at a microscopic level to provide an accurate diagnosis.

2. Natural Strategies: Building a Strong Foundation

While natural methods alone may not reverse genetic balding, they are essential for maintaining a healthy scalp environment:

  • Nutritional Support: Your hair needs protein, iron, zinc, and Vitamin D. Include eggs, leafy greens, nuts, and fish in your diet.
  • Scalp Hygiene: Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo to remove excess oil and environmental pollutants without stripping the scalp of natural oils.
  • Stress Management: High cortisol levels can shift hair follicles prematurely into the shedding phase. Practices like yoga or meditation can help.
  • Gentle Handling: Avoid tight hairstyles and excessive heat styling, which can cause traction alopecia or hair breakage.

3. Medical Treatments: The Heavy Hitters

For visible hair regrowth, medical intervention is often necessary. These are the treatments we recommend most frequently at our clinic:

  • Minoxidil: A FDA-approved topical solution that improves blood flow to the hair follicles, extending the growth phase.
  • Oral Medications: Drugs like Finasteride or Dutasteride (for men) and certain hormonal modulators (for women) can block the DHT hormone responsible for follicle miniaturization.
  • Peptide Serums: Modern alternatives or adjuncts to Minoxidil that use signaling molecules to stimulate follicle activity.

4. Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP) Therapy

PRP treatment in Mangaluru has become incredibly popular due to its high success rate. By injecting concentrated growth factors from your own blood back into the scalp, we can "wake up" dormant follicles and significantly increase hair thickness and density without surgery.

5. When to Seek Professional Help

If you are losing more than 100 strands a day, seeing visible patches of scalp, or experiencing persistent itching and flaking, it's time to consult a dermatologist. Early treatment is much more effective than trying to regrow hair after the follicles have completely scarred over.
