How Laser Hair Removal Works: The Science of Smooth Skin

Laser hair removal works through a process called Selective Photothermolysis. Our skin specialist in Mangaluru explains the biological mechanism:

  • Melanin Targeting: The laser emits a concentrated beam of light that is absorbed by the melanin (pigment) in the hair follicle.
  • Thermal Damage: The light energy converts into heat, which precisely damages the hair follicle without harming the surrounding skin tissue.
  • Growth Inhibition: A damaged follicle can no longer produce new hair. Since hair grows in cycles (Anagen, Catagen, and Telogen), multiple sessions are required to target all hairs in their active growth phase.

Patient FAQs: Expert Answers for Laser Care

How many sessions will I need?

Most patients achieve 80-90% hair reduction in 6 to 8 sessions. The exact number depends on your hair thickness, skin color, and the area being treated.

Can I have laser treatment if I have PCOS?

Yes! Laser is highly recommended for PCOS-related hair growth. However, we combine the laser sessions with medical management to ensure the hormones are balanced, leading to much better and faster results.

Will my hair ever grow back?

Laser provides "permanent hair reduction." While most hair is gone forever, some fine, light-colored hairs may remain. We recommend a quick maintenance session once every 12 months to keep your skin perfectly smooth.

What is the gap between two laser sessions?

For facial hair, sessions are usually spaced 4-6 weeks apart. For body hair, the gap is typically 6-8 weeks, as body hair grows at a slower rate than facial hair.
