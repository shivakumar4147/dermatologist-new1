Understanding Hyperpigmentation: Identifying the Root Cause

A successful treatment plan starts with identifying the specific type of pigmentation. Our skin specialist in Mangaluru performs a detailed clinical analysis to differentiate between various conditions:

  • Melasma: Symmetrical brown or grayish patches, often appearing on the cheeks, forehead, and upper lip. It is primarily driven by hormonal changes and sunlight.
  • Solar Lentigines (Sun Spots): Flat, brown spots caused by long-term UV exposure, common in the intense Mangaluru sun.
  • Post-Inflammatory Hyperpigmentation (PIH): Dark marks left behind after an injury or inflammatory condition like acne or eczema has healed.
  • Dark Circles (Periorbital Hyperpigmentation): Pigmentation under the eyes caused by a combination of genetics, lifestyle, and thinning skin.
  • Freckles & Ephelides: Small, light brown spots that become more prominent with sun exposure.

The Science of Pigmentation: Why Do Dark Spots Form?

Hyperpigmentation occurs when melanocytes (pigment-producing cells) become overactive. Our Mangaluru skin clinic addresses the primary triggers:

  1. UV Radiation: Sunlight is the most significant trigger, stimulating melanin as a protective response.
  2. Hormonal Triggers: Fluctuations during pregnancy (the 'mask of pregnancy') or due to oral contraceptives can lead to melasma.
  3. Inflammatory Response: Traumatic events to the skin trigger a defensive melanin surge.
  4. Systemic Oxidative Stress: Internal health factors can influence skin clarity from within.

Patient FAQs: Expert Answers for Pigmentation Care

Is pigmentation treatment safe for my skin type?

Absolutely. At Skin 360, we specialize in treating Indian skin tones. We use specific laser wavelengths and peels that are clinically proven to be safe and effective, minimizing the risk of adverse reactions.

How long does it take to see results?

Surface spots may show improvement within 2-4 weeks. Deeper conditions like melasma require 3-6 months of consistent treatment, as we need to work with the skin's natural cell turnover cycle.

Will my dark spots come back?

Pigmentation is a chronic condition for many. While we can clear existing spots, new ones can form with sun exposure or hormonal changes. We provide a maintenance routine and strict sun protection rules to keep your skin clear.

Is there any downtime after a laser session?

Most of our laser and peel treatments have minimal downtime. You may experience slight redness or mild flaking for 2-3 days, but you can return to your normal routine almost immediately with proper sun protection.
