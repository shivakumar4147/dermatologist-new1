Common Allergic Skin Conditions We Treat

A successful treatment plan starts with an accurate diagnosis. Our skin specialist in Mangaluru specializes in managing a wide range of allergic presentations:

  • Atopic Dermatitis (Eczema): A chronic condition that makes your skin red and itchy. We focus on rebuilding the skin's genetic barrier defect.
  • Contact Dermatitis: Rashes caused by direct contact with specific substances like nickel, cosmetics, or cleaning agents.
  • Urticaria (Hives): Sudden, itchy welts that can be triggered by food, heat, stress, or infections. We specialize in both acute and chronic urticaria management.
  • Seborrheic Dermatitis: Often manifesting as stubborn dandruff or red, scaly patches on the face and chest.
  • Drug Eruptions: Identifying and managing allergic reactions to medications, which can sometimes be severe.
  • Angioedema: Swelling that occurs deep beneath the skin, often around the eyes or lips, requiring urgent medical intervention.

The Science of Allergy: How We Identify Your Triggers

At Skin 360, we utilize advanced medical testing to stop the guesswork. Every allergy evaluation at our Mangaluru skin clinic may include:

  1. Advanced Patch Testing: The gold standard for contact allergies. We apply standardized series of common allergens to your back for 48-72 hours to identify delayed immune reactions.
  2. Skin Prick Testing: Used for identifying immediate allergies to environmental factors like dust mites, pollen, or certain foods.
  3. Serum IgE Blood Panels: Measuring specific antibodies to confirm systemic allergic sensitivities.
  4. Skin Barrier Analysis: Assessing the level of transepidermal water loss (TEWL) to determine the severity of eczema.

Patient FAQs: Expert Answers for Allergy Care

Is my skin allergy caused by my diet?

While food can trigger hives (urticaria), it is rarely the primary cause of adult eczema. However, we do look for specific food-skin links during our diagnostic process to ensure nothing is missed.

How long does a patch test take?

A full patch test requires three visits over one week (usually Monday, Wednesday, and Friday) to allow enough time for delayed allergic reactions to manifest and be accurately read by the doctor.

Are steroids dangerous for my skin?

When used under strict medical supervision for short periods, steroids are safe and effective. However, we prioritize non-steroidal 'barrier repair' and 'immunomodulators' for long-term maintenance to avoid any side effects.

Can I use normal soap if I have eczema?

Most commercial soaps are too alkaline and strip the skin's natural oils. We recommend specialized 'syndet' bars or soap-free cleansers that maintain your skin's natural pH and moisture barrier.
