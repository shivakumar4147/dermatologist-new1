Common Types of Hair Loss We Treat

A successful treatment plan starts with an accurate diagnosis. At our Mangaluru dermatology clinic, we specialize in managing a wide range of hair and scalp conditions:

  • Androgenetic Alopecia (Pattern Baldness): The most common form of hair loss, driven by genetics and DHT hormones. We provide advanced medical and procedural interventions to slow progression and stimulate regrowth.
  • Telogen Effluvium (Excessive Shedding): Often triggered by stress, illness, or post-pregnancy. We focus on identifying the trigger and providing the nutritional and clinical support needed for rapid recovery.
  • Alopecia Areata (Patchy Hair Loss): An autoimmune condition where the body attacks its own hair follicles. We use specialized medical therapies and localized injections to encourage hair to return.
  • Traction Alopecia: Hair loss caused by tight hairstyles or chemical treatments. We help restore damaged follicles and provide guidance on healthy hair care habits.
  • Scalp Disorders: Conditions like seborrheic dermatitis (chronic dandruff) and scalp psoriasis can hinder hair growth. We treat the scalp environment to ensure optimal follicle health.

The Science of Hair Growth: Our Diagnostic Approach

At Skin 360, we don't guess—we diagnose. Every patient undergoing hair loss treatment in Mangaluru receives a detailed evaluation:

  1. Digital Trichoscopy: Using a high-resolution dermatoscope to examine the hair density, follicle health, and scalp condition at 50x magnification.
  2. Nutritional Blood Profiling: Checking for essential markers like Ferritin (Iron), Vitamin D, B12, and Thyroid levels, which are the building blocks of healthy hair.
  3. Hormonal Assessment: Identifying imbalances in androgens or other hormones that may be miniaturizing your hair follicles.
  4. Scalp Health Analysis: Assessing the presence of inflammation, oiliness, or fungal activity that may be contributing to hair fall.

Patient FAQs: Expert Answers for Hair Care

How long does it take to see results from hair treatment?

Hair grows in cycles, so patience is key. Most patients notice a reduction in hair fall within 4-6 weeks, with visible regrowth and increased density appearing after 3-4 months of consistent treatment.

Is PRP therapy painful?

We prioritize patient comfort. We use high-quality topical numbing creams and very fine needles to ensure the procedure is virtually painless. Most patients describe it as a minor "pinprick" sensation.

Can women also undergo PRP and Mesotherapy?

Absolutely. Female pattern thinning is a very common concern, and these treatments are highly effective for women. We customize the growth factor concentrations specifically for female physiology.

Will I need to take medications forever?

Not necessarily. While some conditions like genetic balding require long-term maintenance, many forms of hair loss can be stabilized, allowing us to eventually reduce or stop oral medications once the hair is healthy.
