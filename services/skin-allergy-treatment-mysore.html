Expert Diagnosis and Relief for Skin Allergies

Skin allergies are a common and often frustrating condition that can significantly impact your quality of life. In the coastal city of Mysore, environmental factors like high humidity, dust, and coastal pollutants can often trigger or worsen skin reactions. At Skin 360 Dermatology Clinic, we provide a comprehensive approach to diagnosing and treating a wide range of allergic skin conditions, ensuring you find long-term relief and restored skin health.

Common Allergic Conditions We Treat

  • Contact Dermatitis: Red, itchy rashes caused by direct contact with allergens like nickel, fragrances, or certain chemicals.
  • Urticaria (Hives): Raised, itchy welts that can appear suddenly due to various triggers, including food, medications, or stress.
  • Atopic Dermatitis (Eczema): A chronic condition that makes skin red and itchy, often seen in individuals with a family history of allergies.
  • Drug Eruptions: Allergic reactions to specific medications that can manifest as rashes or more severe systemic symptoms.
  • Angioedema: Swelling that occurs beneath the skin surface, often around the eyes or lips, sometimes associated with hives.

Our Diagnostic Approach

Identifying the exact trigger is the most crucial step in managing skin allergies. Our Mysore skin specialist uses advanced diagnostic tools:

  • Detailed Medical History: A thorough discussion about your symptoms, lifestyle, and potential exposures.
  • Patch Testing: A specialized test where small amounts of common allergens are applied to the skin to identify delayed allergic reactions.
  • Skin Prick Testing: Used for immediate allergic reactions, often helpful in identifying food or environmental triggers.
  • Blood Tests (IgE): Measuring specific antibody levels to confirm allergic sensitivities.

Personalized Treatment and Barrier Restoration

Effective treatment goes beyond just applying a cream. We focus on a multi-modal strategy to calm inflammation and rebuild your skin's natural defense system:

1. Immediate Symptom Relief

We utilize medical-grade topical corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, and oral antihistamines to rapidly reduce itching, redness, and swelling during an active flare.

2. Barrier Repair Therapy

Chronic allergies often damage the skin barrier. We prescribe specialized medical moisturizers (emollients) containing ceramides and lipids to restore the skin's integrity and prevent future irritants from penetrating.

3. Trigger Avoidance Counseling

Once your triggers are identified through testing, we provide a detailed "safe product list" and lifestyle guidance to help you avoid future exposures in your daily environment.

Frequently Asked Questions: Skin Allergies

What is the difference between a skin allergy and an irritation?

An allergy is an immune system reaction to a specific substance (allergen), whereas an irritation is a physical or chemical damage to the skin's surface. Allergies often cause itching and can spread beyond the contact site, while irritations usually cause burning or stinging and stay localized.

Can skin allergies develop suddenly in adulthood?

Yes, it is possible to develop an allergy to a substance you have used for years. This is known as "sensitization," where the immune system gradually starts recognizing a common substance as a threat over repeated exposures.

How long does patch testing take?

Patch testing typically requires three visits over the course of a week. The patches are applied on Monday, removed and initially read on Wednesday, and a final reading is performed on Friday to catch delayed reactions.
